How It Works

From Citizen Experience
to National Accountability

Five steps that convert everyday service experiences into rankings powerful enough to move institutions.

01
📱

Citizens Rate Services

Via website, mobile, or our toll-free hotline — any Ghanaian can rate a public institution and share their experience.

02
🧑‍🎓

Youth Teams Collect Data

Trained NSS personnel and university SRC students conduct in-person surveys, mystery client assessments, and spot checks nationwide.

🔬

Independent Analysis

Our technical team applies a transparent, weighted scoring methodology validated by an independent Advisory Council.

04
📊

Rankings Published

Annual national report and rankings released at a press conference, briefing Parliament, ministries, media, and development partners.

05
🔄

Institutions Improve

Media scrutiny, parliamentary questions, and public pressure drive institutions to reform. Standards rise. The cycle repeats.

Why GPSSI Matters

Five Ways Rankings
Create Real Change

📡

Public Visibility

Red and Black tier institutions are amplified by media. There is no hiding from a published ranking that citizens can see and share.

🏛️

Political Pressure

Ministers, boards, and CEOs respond to public embarrassment. Parliamentary committees use GPSSI data for scrutiny and accountability.

🏆

Healthy Competition

When ECG is ranked against GWCL, or GCB against ADB, institutions compete to improve. Gold Tier becomes a prize worth chasing.

🪞

Reputation Management

Institutions care about how citizens perceive them. Black Tier status creates internal urgency to reform — before the next rating cycle.

👥

Citizen Empowerment

When citizens see their feedback trigger rankings, press coverage, and reforms, participation grows. The accountability loop strengthens.

📈

Evidence-Based Reform

GPSSI gives government, Parliament, and donors the independent data they need to design targeted service delivery reforms.

About GPSSI

Built by Youth.
For Every Ghanaian.

We are the Movement for Youth Development — a registered Ghanaian civil society organisation on a mission to make public institutions truly accountable to the citizens they serve.

🎯
Our Mission
To empower Ghanaian youth and citizens to collect, analyse, and publish independent satisfaction data on public institutions — generating the pressure and incentives needed for genuine service delivery reform.
👁️
Our Vision
A Ghana where every public institution is measured, ranked, and held accountable to the citizens it serves — by citizens themselves.
⚖️
Our Promise
Non-partisan. Evidence-based. Youth-led. No political party, assessed institution, or government ministry has any role in our data collection, analysis, or rankings.
Our Story

Why We Started GPSSI

Every day, millions of Ghanaians interact with public institutions — hospitals, passport offices, police stations, utilities providers. These interactions shape whether citizens trust their government and believe democracy works for them.

Yet no independent, citizen-driven system existed to measure or report on these experiences. Government ministries had internal data. Audit reports existed. But citizens had no structured voice — and institutions had no external performance pressure to improve.

MYD Ghana created GPSSI to fill that gap. We believe that when institutions know they are being watched, measured, and publicly ranked — they improve. Our model is simple: collect citizen data honestly, publish rankings transparently, and let public visibility do its work.

We are not here to embarrass institutions. We are here to give them every reason to be excellent.

What We Stand For

Our Core Values

Six principles that guide everything GPSSI does.

01
🎯

Independence

No government agency, political party, or assessed institution has any role in our data collection, analysis, or scoring decisions.

02
🔬

Transparency

All methodology, scoring criteria, and summary data are publicly disclosed. Our rankings are reproducible by anyone with the same data.

03
🤝

Youth Agency

Young Ghanaians are not just beneficiaries of governance reform — they are its architects. Our youth networks are the backbone of GPSSI.

04
📢

Constructive Pressure

Rankings are designed to create visibility and incentives that drive genuine improvement — not to shame, but to motivate excellence.

05
🌍

Inclusivity

From rural villages to urban centres, every Ghanaian's experience counts. Our multi-channel approach reaches citizens who cannot go online.

06
📈

Evidence Over Opinion

We let data speak. Every ranking, every scorecard, and every recommendation is grounded in evidence from real citizens.
